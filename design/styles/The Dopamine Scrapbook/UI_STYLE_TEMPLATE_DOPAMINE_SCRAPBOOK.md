We use a "Mixed Media" approach. Headers should feel like they were cut out of a magazine, while body text remains legible but brutalist.

## üß© Component Styling

### 1. The "Scrapbook" Recipe Card
* **Media:** Video-First Backgrounds. The background is a looping, muted video of the food (e.g., cheese bubbling).
* **Elements:** "Dopamine Decor." Use stickers, grainy gradients, and collage-style image layering.
* **Overlays:** Recipe titles use a "tape" background (a black box with 80% opacity) to mimic a label maker.

### 2. Interaction & Feedback ("Juicy")
* **Feedback:** Every tap triggers a micro-animation (e.g., confetti explodes when you save a recipe).
* **Navigation:** Gesture-based. No "Back" buttons; users swipe everything away.

### 3. Navigation Bar
* **Style:** Floating "Island" at the bottom with a frosted glass (blur) effect.
* **Icons:** Thick-stroke neon icons that "glow" when active.

## üñºÔ∏è Screen Inventory Adaptations

### 1. Menu Tab (The Planning Hub)
* **Empty State:** Instead of a simple icon, use a grainy, high-contrast collage of a kitchen in "Acid Green" duotone.
* **Progress Bar:** A thick "Hyper-Pink" neon tube that fills up with a glowing gradient as you cook.

### 2. Recipe Detail (The Reading View)
* **Hero Image:** Uses a "sticker" treatment where the main food item is cut out from its background and layered over a Grainy Gradient.
* **Steps:** Numbering is massive (72pt Syne) and semi-transparent behind the text.
